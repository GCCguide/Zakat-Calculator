1. What is Zakat?

Zakat is the third pillar of Islam — a mandatory annual payment of 2.5% of accumulated wealth above a minimum threshold (Nisab), made to those in need. It is not a charity donation; it is an obligation (fard) on every Muslim who meets the conditions.

Conditions for Zakat to be obligatory:

  • You are Muslim, free, and of sound mind
  • Your net wealth reaches or exceeds the Nisab
  • One full lunar (Hijri) year has passed on that wealth — this is called Hawl

2. Nisab — The Minimum Threshold

Nisab is the minimum amount of wealth you must hold before Zakat becomes due. There are two Nisab standards:

StandardWeightWhy it matters
Gold Nisab85 grams of pure goldScholars recommend this today
Silver Nisab595 grams of silverLower threshold — more conservative
💡 Which to use? Most contemporary scholars recommend the gold Nisab for general wealth. The silver Nisab is sometimes used to be more cautious (includes more people). Use our calculator's Nisab checker at the top to see today's threshold in your currency.

3. Zakat on Cash & Bank Savings

This is the simplest category. Add up all liquid money you hold:

  • Cash at home
  • Current/checking account balances
  • Savings account balances
  • Money owed to you that you expect to be repaid

Subtract: any debts you owe that are currently due.

Apply 2.5% to the net amount. Example: If you have SAR 50,000 in savings and owe SAR 5,000 in rent, your zakatable amount = SAR 45,000 → Zakat = SAR 1,125.

4. Zakat on Gold & Silver

Multiply the weight of gold you own by the current gold price per gram, then apply 2.5%.

What about jewellery?

The majority opinion (Hanafi, Maliki, and one Hanbali view) is that jewellery worn by women is zakatable. The Shafi'i and one Hanbali view says it is exempt if worn regularly. To be safe and follow the majority, include it.

📌 Formula: (Grams of gold × price per gram × 2.5%) + (Grams of silver × silver price per gram × 2.5%)

5. Zakat on Business Inventory

Business Zakat (Zakat al-Tijarah) applies to goods held for resale, not to fixed assets like machinery, buildings, or vehicles used in operations.

Formula: (Market value of inventory + trade receivables + business cash) − current trade debts = zakatable base → × 2.5%

6. Zakat on Stocks & Investments

This depends on your investment intent:

TypeZakat Method
Shares held for trading2.5% of full market value on due date
Long-term investment shares2.5% of your share of company's zakatable assets (cash, inventory, receivables)
Islamic mutual funds / Sukuk2.5% of market value
Unpaid dividendsInclude as cash

8. Common Questions

Do I pay Zakat if I have debts?

You may deduct debts that are currently due from your zakatable wealth. Future debts (like a long-term mortgage) are generally not deducted in full — only the current installment due.

When do I pay Zakat?

One lunar year after your wealth first reached Nisab. Many Muslims choose Ramadan for ease of remembering, but you should pay it on your personal due date.

What if my wealth fluctuates during the year?

The majority view: Zakat is calculated on what you own at the end of the Hawl (one lunar year). Midyear fluctuations don't affect the calculation as long as you held at least Nisab at the start and end of the year.
